🌑 Facts About the Moon

The Moon orbits the Earth. Tap the tiles to learn more!

🔄 The Moon's Orbit Animation
The Moon takes 29 days to go around the Earth once.
⭕
What shape is the Moon's orbit?
The Moon's orbit is a circle (oval shape). The Moon goes all the way around the Earth!
📅
How long does one orbit take?
The Moon takes 29 days to orbit (go around) the Earth once. That is almost one month!
🪨
What are craters? How are they made?
Craters are dents on the Moon's surface. They are made when space rocks crash into the Moon at very high speed!
👨‍🚀
Has a human been to the Moon?
Yes! Neil Armstrong was the first person to walk on the Moon in 1969. The Moon is the only place in space humans have ever been!
💡
Does the Moon make its own light?
No! The Moon does not make its own light. It reflects (bounces) light from the Sun — like a mirror!
⬇️
Does the Moon have gravity?
Yes! The Moon has gravity, but it is weaker than Earth's gravity. On the Moon you can jump very high and float down slowly!

❓ Answer these questions:
1. What shape is the Moon's orbit? ▼
The Moon's orbit is an oval (ellipse) shape — like a slightly squashed circle! The Earth is near the middle.
2. What is being the Moon in Sofia's string model? ▼
The paper ball is being the Moon. It moves in a circle — just like the real Moon moves around the Earth!
3. What does the string represent in the model? ▼
The string represents gravity. Gravity is the force that keeps the Moon moving around the Earth — just like the string keeps the paper ball moving in a circle!
4. Why does the Moon not fly away into space? ▼
Because gravity always pulls the Moon towards the Earth! Even though the Moon is moving fast, gravity keeps pulling it back — so it keeps going around and around!
